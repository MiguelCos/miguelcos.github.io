Drug-response complexome screening

This viewer summarizes a SEC-MS complexome profiling analysis aimed at discovering protein assembly perturbations after HSP90 inhibition. In this approach, native protein assemblies are separated by size-exclusion chromatography and quantified by mass spectrometry across fractions. Changes in elution shape, peak position, and apparent assembly size can reveal drug-associated remodeling of protein complexes even when total protein abundance changes are limited.

This is a prototypical presentation and does not represent scientific claims about the dataset. It is intended to showcase the establishment of a scientific question, extraction of relevant public data, processing into analysis-ready complexome profiles, and presentation in an explorable format for collaboration partners or clients.

Example dataset

The showcase uses public processed data from HT29 human colon adenocarcinoma cells treated with DMSO/control or the HSP90 inhibitor tanespimycin / 17-AAG. The source study is Native Size-Exclusion Chromatography-Based Mass Spectrometry Reveals New Components of the Early Heat Shock Protein 90 Inhibition Response Among Limited Global Changes.

ProteomeXchange / PRIDE: PXD033459. DOI: 10.1016/j.mcpro.2022.100485.

Pipeline approach

The workflow imports processed MaxQuant protein and peptide tables, maps SEC fractions and treatment labels, normalizes quantitative profiles, calls protein/proteoform-level profile features with the reference complexome pipeline, ranks proteins by an assembly perturbation score, and exports a static browser viewer for profile, peptide-map, and condition-network exploration.

The assembly perturbation score is an exploratory ranking metric combining center-of-mass shifts, peak-fraction shifts, and profile-shape distance between Control and HSP90i mean profiles. It is intended for screening and prioritization, not as a publication-grade statistical test.

Viewer tabs

Screening shows ranked protein profiles in groups of five by assembly perturbation score, with Control and HSP90i mean profiles, SD shading, and dashed peak guides to highlight apparent assembly shifts.

Profiles provides a detailed view for the selected protein, including proteoform-level facets when multiple proteoform calls are available and condition-level mean +/- SD traces.

Peptide Map maps detected peptides onto the selected protein/proteoform context, including modification and UniProt reference PTM annotations where available.

Network shows high-scoring pairs of profile features whose co-elution supports the idea of potential complex association. In the context of this assay, it is useful to explore which putative complexes or co-eluting feature pairs may be lost, gained, or changed between Control and HSP90i. This network view is completely exploratory and should be treated as a hypothesis-generation layer, not as evidence of direct physical interaction.
